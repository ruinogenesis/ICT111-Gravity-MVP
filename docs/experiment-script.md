# Experiment Script — Gravity

## 1. Opening Script
"Thank you for helping us test our prototype. This is not a test of you — we are testing whether our marketplace idea and interface are clear. Please think aloud while you use it: say what you're looking for, what you expect to happen, and anything that confuses you. There are no wrong answers, and honest criticism helps us the most."

## 2. Test Scenario
"Imagine the semester is ending. You (or your friend) are moving out of the dorm and need to sell a desk and some textbooks within a few days. Other students are moving in and looking for cheap used items. You'll use Gravity, a secondhand marketplace for RSU students only."

Demo link: see `/prototype/mvp-demo-link.md`. Tester uses their own phone if possible (tests FR-14).

## 3. Tasks for Tester

| Task No. | Task Instruction | Related Requirement | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| T1 | Open the homepage. In your own words, what is this website for, and who can use it? | FR-01 | Tester states: buy/sell used items, RSU students only |
| T2 | You need to sell your study desk (800 baht, good condition, pickup at Dorm A). Post it as a listing. | FR-03, FR-10, FR-11 | Tester completes the form, sees the success confirmation, and finds their desk in the listings |
| T3 | You're looking for a cheap textbook. Find a textbook that costs less than 300 baht. | FR-05, FR-06 | Tester uses search or the Textbooks category filter and identifies GL-003 (ICT111 textbook, 250 THB) |
| T4 | Open that textbook's detail page. Would you trust this seller? What tells you that? | FR-07, FR-15 | Tester opens the detail view and mentions the "RSU verified" badge |
| T5 | Look at the listings. Which items can you still get, and which are gone? What do you think "Reserved" means? | FR-08 | Tester correctly interprets Available / Reserved / Sold |
| T6 | You want the mini fridge. Press "Request contact". What do you think happens next — and why can't you see the seller's LINE ID yet? | FR-02, FR-15 | Tester explains: the seller must accept first, then contact details are revealed to both — and ideally frames this as protection |

## 4. Observation Notes
For each task record in `/data/experiment-results.csv`: completed (Yes/No), time in seconds, help needed (Yes/No), exact confusion point or wrong click, clarity rating 1–5, and improvement note. Do not help unless the tester is fully stuck — if you do, mark "help needed = Yes".

## 5. Closing Questions (neutral — do not lead)
- What was easy to understand?
- What was confusing or unclear?
- Which feature would matter most to you personally, if any?
- What is missing that you would need before using this for real?
- If you were selling something next semester, what would you actually do first — post here, or post in a Facebook/LINE group? Why?
